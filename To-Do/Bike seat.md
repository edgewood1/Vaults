## **A More Detailed, Conversational Approach**

This approach breaks down the question into a conversation, which can often lead to a more thorough answer.
**1. Introduce the Bike and the Goal:**
"Hi, I have a Cannondale Topstone gravel bike, and I'm looking for a solution to carry my child on the back. I know this can be tricky depending on the model."
**2. State the Critical Weight Constraint Upfront:**
"My main challenge is that my child weighs [state the actual weight, e.g., 55 pounds], and I know this is above the 48-pound limit of most child seats. Safety is my number one priority."
**3. Ask About the Specific Solution You Have in Mind:**
"My bike has a **12x142mm thru-axle** and might not have traditional eyelets for a rack. I've read about special systems where the rack mounts directly to a replacement thru-axle. Is that something you're familiar with, and is it a safe option for this bike?"
**4. Ask for Their Expert Recommendation and Concerns:**
"Given the bike's thru-axle system and my child's weight, what solution, if any, would you recommend? I'm also concerned about the **total weight capacity of the bike frame itself** and how a 50-70 pound load on the back would affect the bike's **stability and handling**."
