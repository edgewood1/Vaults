# Christian Traditions

## Catholic vs. Protestant — Authority & Scripture

- **Sola Scriptura (Protestant):** Bible is the sole infallible source of truth. No human (including a pope) or council is error-free.
- **Catholic:** Bible functions alongside Church tradition and the Pope. Authority rests on three pillars: Scripture, Sacred Tradition, and the Magisterium (teaching authority of the Church).

## Catholic — Sacramental Form / Analogical Imagination

- God is present in and through the material world
- Creation is the vehicle that communicates God
- Because the infinite God becomes finite (via the Incarnation), finite things can mediate grace
- It is not God OR the world — it is God IN the world
- Thus Catholicism embraces image, ritual, and material sacrament as real channels of grace

## Rome vs. Orthodox — The 1054 Schism

**Main issue:** Who ran the Church

**The spark — the Filioque:** Latin clause "and the Son" added to the Nicene Creed

**The question:** Where does the Holy Spirit proceed from?
- **Orthodox:** From the Father alone — the Father is the apex of the triangle from which the Son is begotten and the Spirit proceeds
- **Rome:** From the Father *and the Son* (Filioque) — the Spirit proceeds from both
