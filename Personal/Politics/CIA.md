==The Central Intelligence Agency (CIA) works with technology companies in a number of ways, including investing in startups, acquiring technology, and collaborating on research==. The CIA's goal is to use technology to support its mission, which includes national security. 

How the CIA works with tech companies

- **Investing in startups**
    
    The CIA invests in startups through its nonprofit venture capital firm, In-Q-Tel. In-Q-Tel invests in companies that could be useful to the intelligence community within three years. 
    
- **Acquiring technology**
    
    The CIA has acquired technology from companies like Keyhole, Inc., which later became Google Earth. 
    
- **Collaborating on research**
    
    The CIA collaborates with academic institutions and labs to conduct research and experiments. 
    
- **Using commercial software**
    
    The CIA uses commercial software products, including internet-scale software. 
    

Examples of CIA-backed tech companies 

- **Keyhole**: The satellite mapping app that became Google Earth
- **[[Palantir]]**: The data-analytics firm
- **Contour Energy Systems**: The maker of battery technology
- **Mersive**: The supplier of high-resolution displays
- **Reversing Lab**: The developer of computer security technology
- **Databricks**: The data warehousing and AI platform
- **Looxci**: The maker of a "wear and share" video camera
