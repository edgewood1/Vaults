<u>**Here are 5 key areas to research when evaluating a company for stock investment, especially when comparing between companies:**</u>

1. **Financial Health:**	* **Profitability:** Look at the company's income statement. Are they consistently making profits? Are profit margins (like net income margin) strong and improving? Check Income Statement: (Key Statistics: <u>[https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/AAPL/key-statistics?p=AAPL](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/AAPL/key-statistics?p=AAPL)</u> or Financials: <u>[https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/AAPL/financials?p=AAPL](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/AAPL/financials?p=AAPL)</u> tab)
		* Look for: Net Income, Gross Profit Margin, Operating Margin
	* **Debt Levels:** Check their balance sheet. How much debt do they have relative to their assets (debt-to-equity ratio)? High debt can make a company vulnerable in a downturn.  **Debt Levels check Balance Sheet:** (Financials: <u>[https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/AAPL/financials?p=AAPL](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/AAPL/financials?p=AAPL)</u> tab)
		* Look for: Total Debt, Debt-to-Equity Ratio
	* **Cash Flow:** Healthy cash flow indicates a company can meet its financial obligations and fund growth. Look for positive operating cash flow. **Cash Flow Statement:** (Financials: <u>[https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/AAPL/cash-flow?p=AAPL](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/AAPL/cash-flow?p=AAPL)</u> tab)
		* Look for: Operating Cash Flow
2. **Growth Potential:**	* **Revenue Growth:** Is the company's top line (revenue) growing consistently over time? This suggests strong demand for its products or services.
	* **Market Share:** Is the company gaining market share in its industry or is it facing stiff competition? A growing market share is a good sign.
	* **Future Outlook:** Consider the company's plans. Do they have new products in development, expansion strategies, or potential for entering new markets?
* **Market Share & Future Outlook:**

		* Search for company announcements, industry reports, analyst opinions.
* **Revenue Growth:**
	* **Income Statement:** (Key Statistics: <u>[https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/AAPL/key-statistics?p=AAPL](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/AAPL/key-statistics?p=AAPL)</u> or Financials: <u>[https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/AAPL/financials?p=AAPL](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/AAPL/financials?p=AAPL)</u> tab)
		* Look for: Revenue, and its growth over several years
3. **Valuation:**	* **Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:** Compares the stock price to earnings per share. A lower P/E compared to industry peers might suggest the stock is undervalued. (Under 25 is considered good)
	* **Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:** Compares stock price to the value of the company's assets. Can help spot undervalued companies.
	* **PEG Ratio:** Takes the P/E ratio and factors in earnings growth, providing a more nuanced view of valuation.

1. **Management:**	* **Leadership:** Research the CEO and executive team's track record. Do they have a history of success and wise decision-making?
	* **Company Culture:** A positive and innovative culture can attract talent and drive long-term success. Look for information on employee satisfaction and company values.

* **Barriers to Entry** Does the company have a competitive advantage that protects their market (like patents or strong brand recognition)?
* **Industry Trends:** Assess broader factors influencing the industry. Are there favorable trends the company is poised to capitalize on, or potential threats?
