In culinary science, almost every baked good falls into one of three categories based on what "engine" is used to create volume and texture.
1. Biological Leavening (The "Yeast" Category)
 * Key Ingredient: Yeast (a living organism).
 * How it works: The yeast eats sugar and releases carbon dioxide slowly over time. This requires patience (proofing/rising).
 * Examples: Sourdough, baguettes, brioche, pizza dough, cinnamon rolls.
 * Texture: Chewy, elastic, and airy structure (due to gluten development).
2. Chemical Leavening (The "Powder" Category)
 * Key Ingredient: Baking Soda or Baking Powder.
 * How it works: These chemicals react with moisture and heat to create gas bubbles instantly. There is no waiting for the dough to rise; you mix it and bake it immediately (hence the name "Quick Breads").
 * Examples: Muffins, cakes, cookies, biscuits, scones, banana bread.
 * Texture: Tender, crumbly, or cake-like.
3. Mechanical / Physical Leavening (The "Air & Steam" Category)
 * Key Ingredient: Eggs or Butter (manipulated by technique).
 * How it works: There are no artificial leaveners (no yeast, no soda). Instead, you trap air bubbles by whipping eggs, or you create layers of butter that release steam.
 * Examples:
   * Steam-leavened: Puff pastry, croissants, pie crusts (the water in the butter turns to steam and puffs up the layers).
   * Air-leavened: SoufflÃ©s, sponge cakes, angel food cake (whipped egg whites hold the air).
 * Texture: Flaky (pastry) or foamy/light (sponge).
Alternative View: Lean vs. Rich
If you prefer to categorize based on fat rather than the rising agent, bakers also divide doughs into two massive families:
| Category | Key Ingredients | Characteristics |
|---|---|---|
| Lean Doughs | Flour, Water, Salt, Yeast | Hard crust, chewy interior, low calorie. (e.g., French Bread, Pizza) |
| Rich Doughs | Added Fats (Butter, Oil), Sugar, Eggs | Soft crust, tender interior, cake-like. (e.g., Brioche, Dinner Rolls, Muffins) |
Summary for your mental model:
If you want to organize everything in baking, ask: "What makes it rise?"
 * Is it alive? -> Yeast Bread
 * Is it chemical? -> Cake/Cookie/Quick Bread
 * Is it steam/air? -> Pastry/Sponge
