# Dark Eyes (Les Yeux Noirs)

**Key:** D minor · rel: F major · 16 bars · Russian folk / Gypsy jazz

**Scale:** D Harmonic Minor — D E F G A Bb C#  ·  Formula: 1 2 b3 4 5 b6 7

---

## Theory Rules

**The "Home" Rule**
Dm is home. Safe, stable. Target note: D (root) or A (5th).

**The "Tension" Rule**
A7 is tension — it creates the pull. Never play a plain A7 arpeggio. Play C#dim7 instead (C# E G Bb). It hits the 3rd, 5th, 7th, and the b9 (Bb). Do not play the root (A) — let the bass handle it.

**The "Magic Note" — Bb**
Bb is the bridge between chords. In Dm it is the natural 6th. In A7 it is the b9 (dangerous tension). Lean on Bb over A7 to create the "Gypsy Cry."

**The "Diminished Shortcut"**
A7 → play C#dim7. Gm → play Edim7. These are the same notes (inversions of each other). One diminished shape covers 12 out of 16 bars.

## Structure

| Bars | Chord | Arpeggio | Why |
|------|-------|----------|-----|
| 1–4 | A7 | C#dim7 (C# E G Bb) | Setup: starts on tension. Don't play root A. |
| 5–8 | Dm | Dm (D F A) | Release: land on D or F to resolve. |
| 9–12 | Gm | Edim7 (E G Bb C#) | Twist: use diminished shape on the IV chord. |
| 13–14 | A7 | C#dim7 | Return: back to tension. |
| 15–16 | Dm | Dm | End: home base. |

## Turnarounds

**The Diminished Hack (A7 & Gm)**
Use the same shape for both. C#dim7 (for A7) and Edim7 (for Gm) are inversions — same notes. Learn one diminished shape, use it for 12 of 16 bars.

**The Target Note Game**
- Over A7: aim for C# (3rd) or Bb (b9)
- Over Dm: aim for D (root) or F (3rd)

## Practice Routine

**Phase A — The Scale Drill**
Play D Harmonic Minor up and down over the full backing track. Listen to how C# and Bb clash beautifully over A7 and resolve over Dm.

**Phase B — Call & Response (80 BPM)**
- Bars 1–2 (A7): strum A7 on beat 1, rest, then play C#dim7 arpeggio as quarter notes
- Bars 3–4 (Dm): strum Dm on beat 1, rest, then play Dm arpeggio as quarter notes
- Repeat through the full structure

**Phase C — Target Practice**
Play continuous 8th notes from D Harmonic Minor over the backing track. Land on a chord tone on beat 1 of every chord change:
- Changing to A7 → land on C# or Bb
- Changing to Dm → land on D or A

---

## Speed Building

**The Burst Method**
Do not just raise the metronome. Set it slow (80 BPM). Play C#dim7 arpeggio as quarter notes. Next bar: eighth notes. Next bar: triplets. Switch gears without the hand tensing.

**The Lock Method**
Pick a 4-note sequence (e.g., D–E–F–G). Loop it. Focus entirely on the right hand — the pick should be falling through the strings (rest strokes). Speed comes from relaxation, not force.

## Visual Reference

**The 5th Position Box (frets 4–7)**
- Dm root: 5th fret (A string)
- A7 root: 5th fret (E string)
- C# (3rd of A7): 4th fret (A string)
- Bb (tension): 8th fret (D string) or 6th fret (high E)

Everything you need is in frets 4–7. You are always swinging between tension (diminished / Bb) and release (minor / D).

**The Spider Shape (diminished)**
Index on 4, middle on 5, ring on 6 — diagonal shape. Slide it up 3 frets and it repeats. Use this to create movement without changing keys.
