# Swing Gitane (Swing Gitan)

**Key:** G minor · rel: Bb major · 16 bars AA + 2 endings · Anon / trad Gypsy jazz

**Scales:**
- G natural minor: G A Bb C D Eb F
- G harmonic minor: G A Bb C D Eb F# (over A7/D7) · Formula: 1 2 b3 4 5 b6 7
- Gm69 pentatonic: G A Bb D E (soloing)

*Note: This version (associated with Tchavolo Schmitt / Angelo Debarre) replaces the IV chord (Cm) with the II7 chord (A7), creating a "Circle of Fifths" drive (A7 → D7 → Gm).*

---

## Theory

**The "Circle of Fifths" Drive**
A7 → D7 → Gm (V of V → V → I)

**The "Chromatic Slide"**
Gm → Ab → Gm. Ab is a tritone substitution (Neapolitan chord) — exactly one half-step above G, slides down smoothly into resolution.

**The Endings**
- First ending (to repeat): A7 → D7, 2 bars each — keeps energy up
- Second ending (to solo): A7 → D7, 1 bar each; then Gm → D7, 2 bars each

## Practice Routine

**Phase A — The Circle Drill (A7 → D7 → Gm)**
- A7 (bars 3–4): play C#dim7, starts on fret 4 or 5
- D7 (bars 5–6): play F#dim7, starts on fret 2 or 5
- C#dim7 and F#dim7 share no notes — this forces a clear sound switch

**Phase B — The Half-Step Drop (Ab → Gm)**
- Bars 9–10 (Ab): play Ab major triad, root on low E string, 4th fret
- Bar 11 (Gm): slide hand down one fret to G, root on low E string, 3rd fret
- Goal: make it sound like the whole band is falling into the G chord

**Phase C — The Endings**
Loop: play song → take 1st ending → repeat top.
Finish: play song → take 2nd ending → stop.

## Visual Cheat Sheet

**The Diminished Connection**
- Over A7: C#dim7 (x–4–5–3–5–x)
- Over D7: F#dim7 (x–3–4–2–4–x) or (2–x–1–2–1–x)
- Note: if you play C#dim7 on the 5th fret, you can stay on the same fret for F#dim7 (diminished shapes repeat every 3 frets)

**The Ab Power Move**
Grab an Ab barre chord (4th fret) or Ab7 shape and hit it hard. The clash is good — the resolution to G (3rd fret) is all the more satisfying.
