# Limehouse Blues

**Key:** G major · rel: E minor · 32 bars AABA · Philip Braham, 1921

**Scales:**
- G major: G A B C D E F#
- G mixolydian: G A B C D E F (over dominant chords)
- E minor: E F# G A B C D
- Dominant 7th arpeggios / Mixolydian over each chord (formula: 1 3 5 b7)

---

## The Golden Rule of Tension

Choose notes based on where the chord is resolving:

- Resolving to a **major or dominant** chord → use **natural tensions** (9, 13) → bright, happy, swingy
- Resolving to a **minor** chord → use **altered tensions** (b9, b13) → dark, exotic, "Django"

Shortcut for altered tension: play a diminished 7th arpeggio starting a half-step above the root.

## Harmonic Map

| Chord | Function | Rule | What to Play | Secret |
|-------|----------|------|--------------|--------|
| C7 | IV7 | → A7 (dominant) → bright | C E G Bb + D (9) or A (13) | Don't play C# or Eb here. Keep it bright to contrast what comes next. |
| A7 | V of V | → D7 (dominant) → bright | A C# E G + B (9) or F# (13) | Target note: C#. Hit it to prove you left the C7 chord. |
| G | Tonic | home base | G B D + E (6) | G6 arpeggio (G–B–D–E) for classic swing sound. |
| B7 | V of vi | → Em (minor) → dark | Cdim7: C Eb Gb A | Adds b9 (C) and b13 (G) automatically. Pulls strongly to E minor. |
| Em | Minor | relative minor | E G B | Land on G (minor 3rd) to establish the mood. |
| E7 | V of ii | → Am (minor) → dark | Fdim7: F Ab B D | Diminished shape again for the "Gypsy growl." |
| Am | Supertonic | ii | A C E | Keep it simple — prepare for Cm6. |
| Cm6 | Minor IV | → G (major) | C Eb G A | Target: Eb. Resolve down one fret to D when the band hits G. |
| D7 | Dominant V | → C7 or G | D F# A C | C (b7) leads back smoothly to E (3rd of C7) or root of G. |

## Structure (AABA Form)

**A Section:**
```
C7  C7  A7  A7
G   B7  Em  Em
A7  D7  G   G
```
Starts on C7 (IV7) — away from home immediately. The C7 → A7 jump is the signature sound.

**B Section:**
```
C7  C7  A7  A7
G   E7  Am  Am
Cm6 Cm6 G   G
```
Same opening as A, but resolves through Am → Cm6 → G — the emotional peak.

**Chord structure (scale degrees):**
```
A:  4 … 4 … / 2 … 2 …
    6  6  3  6 / 2  2  5  4#
B:  6  6  2  2 – 4  5  3  1
```

## Turnarounds

**The Standard Turnaround (A7 → D7)**
Guide tones: C# (over A7) → C natural (over D7) → B (over G)

**The Minor Turnaround (B7 → Em)**
Diminished arpeggios: Cdim7 shape → land on G (Em)

**The Sentimental Ending (Cm6 → G)**
Target Eb. Resolve it down one half-step to D when the band hits G. This is the "Minor Plagal Cadence" — the emotional peak.

## The Django Resolution Lick (Am → Cm6 → G)

Rhythm: swing 8ths · Start slow

```
      Am                 Cm6                   G
e |------------|---------------------|-------------------|
B |------------|-----------4--3------|-------------------|
G |----2--5----|--5--4--5--------5---|--4----------------|
D |-2----------|---------------------|--5----------------|
A |-----3------|---------------------|--5----------------|
E |--5---------|---------------------|--3----------------|
     A  C  E  A  C      C  B  C  Eb D  C      B (target)
```

- Bar 1 (Am): ascending Am arpeggio (A–C–E–A)
- Bar 2 (Cm6): enclose C with B then C; hit Eb (4th fret B string), resolve to D (3rd fret B string)
- Bar 3 (G): land on B (4th fret G string) — confirms major tonality

## Practice Drills

**Drill A — The Jump (C7 → A7)**
The hardest change. C natural → C#. Play continuous 8th notes; land on a guide tone on beat 1 of each change.
- Beat 4 of C7: play C or Bb
- Beat 1 of A7: land on C# or A

**Drill B — The Dark Turn (B7 → Em)**
Apply the tension rule here. Play G major (happy) → Cdim7 over B7 (dark/tense) → resolve to Em (sad).

**Drill C — The Sentimental Ending (Cm6 → G)**
Find Eb on your guitar (B string, 4th fret). Play it over Cm6. Slide it down one fret to D right when the band hits G. Milk it.

**Drill D — Horizontal Connection (C7 → A7 arpeggio)**
Play C7 arpeggio (C–E–G–Bb) ascending. Immediately shift to A7 arpeggio (A–C#–E–G) descending. Goal: connect Bb (flat 7 of C7) smoothly to C# (3rd of A7) or A (root).

## Visual Cheat Sheet

**The Falling Shapes (C7 → A7 → G)**
- C7: root on A string, 3rd fret
- A7: slide same shape down to open A (or E string, 5th fret)
- G: slide down to E string, 3rd fret
- Concept: physical movement down the neck mirrors the harmonic movement

**The Cm6 Trick**
Make a D7 shape at the 3rd fret. Move the B string note up one fret (D → Eb). That is your Cm6. Resolve it back down to end the song.
