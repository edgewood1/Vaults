# Her

When we get play, three attitudes arise — referenced by the phrases: touch, don’t touch. Jump towards and draw back. Lately a third: careful. A table, a wall — be careful.

When she practices care she holds her hands up as if holding the object in balanced perspective. Or as if invisibly balancing. Her gaze is softer — neither acquisitive nor distancing — but rather a wonder of what lays between. This thing on the verge of falling apart and yet somehow holding itself together without hint of its being on the verge.

In the past my life was always this care. Until care slipped away and this play of touch and don’t touch rose to the fore again. Don’t touch this because it will prevent you from achieving y, because you must always chisel away at y. Or must I? How to bring in care if not through quieting this. A care spell cast on whoever might read and respell.
