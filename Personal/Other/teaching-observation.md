# Teaching as Observation

Teaching has more to do with observing processes than imposing forms.

We learn to observe by observing our own process as we maintain artistic threads. We learn to see our own forms morph over time and thus get to know the life cycles of forms — such that we develop an intuition for them. If one form unfolds, it's likely to move in a certain direction; if it's not, it likely needs a degree of influence that nevertheless respects the free will of the unfolding itself.

This is the reason we maintain artistic threads — that we maintain non-productive (non-product-oriented) practices — so that we learn morphology: the study of the lifecycle of forms.
