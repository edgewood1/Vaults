# Mars



1. Mars has two moons: Phobos and Deimos
2. Phobos races around Mars really fast, going around it 3 times each day.
3. Deimos takes its time, going around Mars once every day and a half.
4. Mars is a giant sandbox.  Around half of it is covered by sand and dust.
5. This sand and dust on Mars is red because it contains a lot of rust (iron oxide).
6. Both Mars and Earth have mountains, ice caps, volcanoes, and big valleys.
7. Mars doesn't have much rain like Earth, but it can have dust storms.  It can get really cold at night and hot during the day.
8. Mars is further away from the sun than the Earth, so it takes Mars longer to go around the sun: A year on Mars is actually 687 days! 
9. The Spirit rover explored the surface for 1944 days (that’s almost 6 years!) before getting stuck in the sand. 
10. There have been 10 robots that landed on Mars successfully.  
11. Mars is a lot smaller than Earth.  It is almost half as small.  
12. Mars is sometimes called the “strong planet” because it reminds us to be brave and strong!


Pronunciation

Phobos: Foe - boss
Deimos: Day - moss
