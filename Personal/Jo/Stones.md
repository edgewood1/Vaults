<u>**Picture 2: crystals**</u>

These are almost certainly treated Quartz crystals, commonly known as "Aura Quartz."

The base mineral is natural Quartz (silicon dioxide), which is then treated with a high-heat process to permanently bond various metals to its surface. This process creates the striking iridescent, metallic sheen.

* | Top Left (Red/Pink/Gold Iridescence) Deep pink/red with a metallic, rainbow sheen. | Flame Aura Quartz or Tanzine Aura Quartz | Gold and Niobium (or similar combinations). |

* | Center (Dark Blue/Teal Iridescence) | Deep, saturated blue and green with a metallic sheen. | Aqua Aura Quartz or Peacock Ore Aura | Gold and possibly Indium or Niobium. |

* | Center Right (Clear with White Tip) | Clear/white with a natural, uncolored look. | Natural Quartz Point | Untreated. This is the base material for the others. |

* | Center Bottom (Green/Blue/Clear) | Clear base with a bright green/blue color. | Kiwi Aura Quartz or Angel Aura Quartz (with a green tint) | Platinum and/or Silver. |

* | Bottom (Pale Yellow/Green) | Lightly tinted yellow-green, possibly slightly iridescent. | Prasiolite (though often heat-treated Amethyst) or Lemon Aura Quartz | Likely an Iron-based coating or simple heat treatment. |

Key Takeaways:
 * Mineral: The core is Quartz.
 * Color: The intense, metallic, and non-uniform colors are man-made (not naturally occurring in these shades and luster).
 * Name: They are sold commercially as various types of Aura Quartz (e.g., Aqua Aura, Angel Aura, Flame Aura).

<u>**Elements for stone ID**</u>

 * Color: (e.g., bright pink, milky white, speckled gray)
 * Luster: Is it glassy, dull, waxy, or metallic?
 * Texture/Shape: Is it smooth, rough, crystalline, or layered?
 * Hardness/Weight (Optional): Does it feel heavy for its size? Can you scratch it with a fingernail or a penny? (Note: Do this carefully, as some rocks are brittle.)
