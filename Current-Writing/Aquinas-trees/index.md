## Piece Descriptions

### `1-tree-wind.md` — *The Tree and the Wind* (Zine v6)

The broadest piece. Fourteen sections plus an appendix, moving from simple observation through the complete Aristotelian-Thomistic account of reality to contemplation and the soul's return to God.

- I. Change & the Changeless — the tree in wind; the founding question
- II. The Method — how to approach the hidden: watch how the tree responds
- III. Form — The Cause of Permanence — substantial form as the hidden principle
- IV. Accidental Form — Form Meeting the World — accidental form accumulates biography
- V. Matter — prime matter as radical, silent openness; secondary matter as informed, specific stuff *[working section]*
- VI. On Wholeness — three kinds of whole: perceptual, quantitative, conceptual
- VII. On Substantial and Virtual Form — the tree's form present in every part without being divided
- VIII. The External Force — efficient cause; the wind's origin in temperature differentials
- IX. The Internal Nature — operation and power; the tree's specific response reveals its nature
- X. The Deep Stability — form holds pattern; matter makes it particular
- XI. The Ultimate Reality — essence and existence; even a complete nature is only possibility until existence makes it real
- XII. The Invisible Gardeners — angelology; intelligence as particular as what it governs
- XIII. The Circle of Stewardship — final cause and hierarchy; the chain turns back into care
- XIV. The Center and the Song — contemplation; the tree's existence as ontological prayer
- Appendix — technical glossary

### `4-foundation.md` — *Wound and Welcome*

The rigorous philosophical argument that the pine bark pieces enact phenomenologically. Synthesizes counterspace (Adams and Whicher), Aquinas's *esse*, and object-oriented ontology into a single argument.

- I. What Cannot Be Named Directly — apophatic approach to counterspace; void vs. welcome
- II. The Hierarchy of Principles — counterspace as genus; primary matter, space, time, and potency as species
- III. Counterspace and the Question of Ground — counterspace is itself a creature; *esse* is the act; counterspace is the wound where *esse* enters
- IV. Sensual Object and Withdrawn Object — real object (always withdrawn) vs. sensual object (the offered face)
- V. Shadows Cast by Persons — angelic hierarchies as shadows of the hierarchy of principles; the principium etymology
- VI. What Remains — depth has a depth; the deeper depth is not indifferent

The densest single line: *"Counterspace is the wound in creation where* esse *enters."* Likely finished as a standalone.

---

### `5-plenum.md` — *The Architecture of Fullness*

Systematic Thomistic map covering the whole cosmos from God down to grace.

- I. The Source — God as *Ipsum Esse*; the sun/daylight analogy
- II. The Two Fields — Common Existence and Prime Matter
- III. The Island of Matter — secondary matter, accidental form, movement as creative act
- IV. The Ocean of Spirit — angels as Pure Forms distinguished by intensity
- V. The Human Bridge — humans as hybrid; virtual vs. simple intensity; identity after death
- VI. Grace — supernatural elevation, not natural endowment
- VII. The Fullness — the universe as plenum; no isolated beings

The most teachable piece. Key metaphors: Island of Matter / Ocean of Spirit; primary colors (angels) vs. unique blends (human souls).
