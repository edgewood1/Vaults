## **Project 2025: Remaking Education Through "School Choice" and the Social Credit Economy**
Project 2025, a conservative plan for the next presidential administration, aims to overhaul education through **"school choice"** initiatives. While proponents claim this will empower parents and improve outcomes, critics argue it's a strategy to corporatize education and integrate it into a **social credit economy**.
**Dismantling the Department of Education**
A central goal is to dismantle the Department of Education (ED) and redistribute its functions to other federal agencies. This restructuring would shift responsibilities to entities like the Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) and the Department of Labor (DOL). Rather than reducing federal influence, this could expand government reach into education.
**The "School Choice" Illusion
"School choice"** initiatives, including **Education Savings Accounts (ESAs)** and **tax credits for Scholarship Granting Organizations (SGOs)**, are key components of Project 2025's education strategy. ESAs would allow parents to use public funds for private schooling expenses. Tax credits would incentivize donations to SGOs, which would then provide scholarships for private schools.
However, these initiatives may come with strings attached:
* **Increased Regulation:** Private and home schools accepting ESA funds could face increased government regulation.
* **Public-Private Partnerships:** "School choice" promotes **public-private partnerships**, where private companies manage schools with public funding.
* **Ed-Tech Influence:** A significant portion of ESA funds could be directed toward **ed-tech** products, potentially leading to increased data collection and a focus on technology-driven learning.
**Social Credit and "Pay for Success"**
Project 2025 is intertwined with the rise of a **social credit economy** and **"Pay for Success" (PFS)** models.
* **Data-Mining:** Schools would collect extensive student data, including academic performance, behavior, and socio-emotional information.
* **PFS Contracts:** Funding, including scholarships, could be tied to PFS contracts, where outcomes are measured against specific metrics, including **ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) metrics**.
* **Stakeholder economy**: Big Government, Big Business, and Big Tech would determine what outcomes are desirable.
* **The Fourth Industrial Revolution (4IR):** This data collection supports the stakeholder economy.
**The Koch-SPN Network**
The **Koch-SPN network**, including organizations like the Heritage Foundation, ALEC, and EdChoice, has long advocated for these types of "school choice" policies. This network promotes policies at the state level. Betsy DeVos has worked with ALEC to promote corporate school choice reforms.
**Concerns and Implications**
Critics express concerns that Project 2025's education plans could:
* **Reduce Educational Freedom:** The terms and conditions of "school choice" options may be dictated by corporations and government entities, limiting the autonomy of parents and schools.
* **Promote a Stakeholder economy** Shift the focus of education towards workforce development and social engineering, rather than critical thinking and well-rounded learning.
In conclusion, Project 2025's education agenda represents a significant shift towards corporatized, data-driven education, raising concerns about government overreach, the influence of private interests, and the potential impact on student learning and autonomy.

**Key Terms and Concepts:**
* **Project 2025:** A conservative plan for the next presidential administration, including significant changes to education.
* **School Choice:** Initiatives that allow public funds to be used for private schooling options, such as private schools, charter schools, and homeschooling.
* **Education Savings Accounts (ESAs):** Government-authorized accounts that parents can use to pay for approved educational expenses, including private school tuition, tutoring, and online programs.
* **Tax Credits for Scholarship Granting Organizations (SGOs):** Tax breaks given to corporations or individuals who donate to SGOs, which then provide scholarships for students to attend private schools.
* **Public-Private Partnerships:** Collaborations between government entities and private companies to manage or operate schools and educational programs.
* **Fourth Industrial Revolution (4IR):** The current era of technological advancement characterized by automation, data exchange, and cyber-physical systems.
* **Social Credit Economy:** An economic system where social behavior and data are used to determine access to resources and opportunities.
* **Pay for Success (PFS) / Social Impact Investing:** Funding models where investors provide upfront capital for social programs and are repaid by the government or other entities if the programs achieve predetermined outcomes.
* **ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) Metrics:** Criteria used to evaluate a company's or organization's impact and ethical practices related to environmental, social, and governance factors.
* **Data-Mining:** The practice of collecting and analyzing large amounts of data to identify patterns and insights. In this context, it refers to the collection of student data for various purposes.
* **Ed-Tech:** Educational technology, including software, hardware, and online platforms used in education.
* **State Policy Network (SPN):** A network of conservative and libertarian think tanks that advocate for state-level policies, including school choice.
* **Stakeholder economy:** This refers to an economic system where corporations are expected to serve the interests of all stakeholders, including employees, customers, communities, and the environment, rather than just shareholders.
