I'd appreciate a clear definition of Weird Lit, even from Wikipedia. It seems, superficially, to be any speculative fiction that challenges conventional literary elements.  Here are some additional features I found online: 

Weird fiction often 
* features threats that are not easily categorized or understood, evoking a sense of unease and cosmic dread. These threats may be supernatural, alien, or defy conventional explanation.
* prioritizes creating a sense of atmosphere, often dark, unsettling, and dreamlike. The focus is on evoking emotions like dread, awe, and curiosity rather than relying on jump scares or gore.
* frequently subverts or reinterprets traditional horror tropes, such as ghosts, vampires, and werewolves, or introduces entirely new kinds of monsters and entities.
* has as a common theme cosmic horror, which emphasizes the insignificance of humanity in the face of vast, indifferent cosmic forces and entities
