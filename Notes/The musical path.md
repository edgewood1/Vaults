Now, to your deeper question:

**What is the work of being musical?**

It is not primarily “being included.”
It is not proximity to groups.
It is not visibility.

The work is:
	1.	**Attention** — refining your ear and touch.
	2.	**Presence** — learning to inhabit sound fully.
	3.	**Generosity** — shaping yourself so that when you meet others, you elevate the room.
	4.	**Continuity** — staying with it across seasons.

Regular practice is social because:
	•	You are preparing for future encounters.
	•	You are honoring the lineage of music.
	•	You are participating in something larger than yourself.

Even alone, you are in conversation:
	•	With the tradition.
	•	With past players.
	•	With the version of yourself five years from now.
	•	With whoever will one day sit across from you and play.

That reframes solitude from exclusion to apprenticeship.
